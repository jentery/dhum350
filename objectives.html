By the conclusion of this course, you should learn to:

  • Prototype an indie game that makes an argument about gaming culture, social relations, or systems thinking,
  • Combine critical thinking in the humanities with basic technical competencies in computing and materials design,
  • Iteratively develop, revise, share, discuss, and formally present your protoype with an emphasis on games as systems (not a bundles of unrelated component parts), and
  • Demonstrate an awareness of various strategies used by humanities researchers to prototype games with social and cultural issues in mind.

Student work will be evaluated based on a gaming "manual" (three marks), a presentation (one mark), and a prototype (one mark).
